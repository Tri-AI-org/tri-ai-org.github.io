*September 2020*
 
Two research projects from the AI Saturdays Lagos research track were selected for the **2020 IndabaX-AI4D Innovation Grants**, awarded by Deep Learning Indaba in partnership with the International Development Research Centre (IDRC), the Swedish International Development Cooperation Agency (SIDA), Knowledge 4 All Foundation, and the International Research Centre on Artificial Intelligence under UNESCO.
 
**11 projects were selected from 109 submissions across Africa.** Two of them came out of the AI6 Lagos community.
 
### The selected projects
 
**Improving Online Learning Experience using Accent Transfer.** Reformulating online educational content into local accents so it lands more naturally for learners. *Team: Tejumade Afonja, Munachiso Nwadike, Olumide Okubadejo, Lawrence Francis, Clinton Mbataku, Femi Azeez, and Wale Akinfaderin.* This grant supported the foundational work that later became the [SautiDB-Naija paper](#sautidb-naija-learning-nigerian-accent-embeddings-from-speech) and corpus.
 
**ChexNet Model Compression for Pneumonia Detection on Low-Powered Edge Devices.** Compressing the ChexNet pneumonia detection model so it can run on low-powered devices, broadening access to AI-assisted chest x-ray screening. *Team: Rukayat Sadiq, Brume Love, Jeremiah Fadugba, Olalekan Olapeju, Oluwafemi Azeez, Pelumi Oladokun, and Tella Hambal.*
 
### Why it matters
 
The IndabaX-AI4D grants funded six-month projects supporting African AI research communities through the COVID-19 pandemic, with a focus on the UN Sustainable Development Goals. Being selected at a rate of roughly 10% across the continent put AI6 Lagos on the same shortlist as research teams from Mozambique, Tanzania, Cameroon, Ghana, Kenya, Senegal, Rwanda, and Ivory Coast.
 
The grant recipients presented their work at the [#IndabaSession livestream on 1 September 2020](https://youtu.be/3DwIHe1u8Qg).
