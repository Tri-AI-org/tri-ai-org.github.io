## What it is

ChowNet is a research project building image datasets and recognition models centred on African food. Vision systems trained predominantly on Western culinary imagery struggle to identify staples, preparations, and presentations common across African kitchens. ChowNet aims to close that gap.

## Why it matters

Food recognition powers nutritional tracking, restaurant-discovery products, agricultural research, and dietary public-health work. When the underlying models don't recognise jollof, fufu, ugali, or injera, those downstream applications fail Africans by default.

## What we're doing

Building a curated, geographically diverse image dataset; training and benchmarking recognition models; and releasing both as open resources for the broader research community.
